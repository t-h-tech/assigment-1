Harrat Uwayrid nature reserve Saudi Arabia's second biosphere reserve, situated in the nation's west, provides sanctuary for species at critical risk globally, including the Arabian leopard and Arabian gazelle, along with native plant species. Around 50,000 inhabitants in the surrounding and transitional zones rely primarily on agricultural and pastoral pursuits for their livelihood, such as raising camels, sheep, and goats.

The Sharaan Reserve in AlUla showcases Saudi Arabia's commitment to environmental conservation, focusing on protecting its diverse ecosystems and reviving native flora and fauna. Set in a region famous for its scenic beauty and historical significance, the reserve is part of Vision 2030's broader efforts to balance ecosystems and engage local communities in wildlife management. Despite facing challenges like overgrazing, Sharaan highlights the potential for ecological restoration, emphasizing sustainable practices to ensure the area's natural and cultural heritage is preserved for future generations.

AlUla is home to five nature reserves covering an area of 12,500 square kilometers, with the Wadi Al-Nakhlah, Harrat Al-Zubayr, and Harrat Uwayrid reserves serving as future natural habitats with potential for Arabian leopards.
